# Content templates

Every article on offroading.news follows a strict structure. This keeps the site consistent and scannable on mobile.

## Universal rules

- Reading level: 8th-9th grade
- Always vehicle-specific — never generic
- Honest difficulty and cost assessments
- Active voice ("torque the bolt to 95 lb-ft" not "the bolt should be torqued")
- Sentence case for headlines
- No exclamation points outside safety warnings
- No listicles

## FIX article template

1. **What's wrong** — symptom description, 1-2 sentences
2. **What causes it** — root cause explanation
3. **What you need** — parts list with part numbers and approximate cost
4. **How to fix it** — step-by-step with torque specs, clearances, specific numbers
5. **How to verify** — test procedure after the fix
6. **When to go to a shop** — honest assessment of DIY vs. professional

## UPGRADE article template

1. **Why this upgrade** — what problem it solves or capability it adds
2. **What you need** — parts list with costs, tools required
3. **Compatibility** — what years/trims this works on, what it doesn't
4. **Install steps** — detailed walkthrough
5. **Before/after** — measurable differences (clearance, travel, weight)
6. **Trade-offs** — what you give up (ride quality, fuel economy, warranty)

## TRAIL article template

1. **Overview** — location, length, estimated time
2. **Difficulty** — rating with specific justification (rock size, water depth, incline)
3. **Minimum vehicle requirements** — tire size, clearance, lockers, specific capabilities
4. **The route** — turn-by-turn or section-by-section description
5. **Hazards** — specific dangers with mile markers or GPS coords
6. **Facilities** — fuel, camping, cell service, recovery options

## NEWS article template (AI-curated)

AI-generated summaries follow this structure:
1. **What happened** — 2-3 sentence summary
2. **Why it matters for off-roaders** — relevance to the community
3. **Source attribution** — always link to original source
